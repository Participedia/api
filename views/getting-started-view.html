Getting Started as a Contributor

Anyone from around the world can help document participatory initiatives by publishing and editing crowdsourced entries on participedia.net.

Participedia is a crowdsourcing platform for researchers and practitioners interested in public participation. The site is home to over a thousand cases from nearly 70 countries and has an active community of contributors who use the site to study and design effective methods of citizen engagement. The site is currently available in 7 languages, and features two key types of contributors: Publishers and Editors.

Publishers

Most often people who publish new entries on Participedia are students, practitioners and organizers of participatory events. Students may be assigned course work related to Participedia, either for researching or documenting participatory initiatives, such as this collection contributed by Coady International Centre graduates. Practitioners may use the site to document and publicize their methods, cases and organizations. Organizers who publish entries on Participedia may benefit from increased discoverability of their work, at the same time contributing to a collaborative effort to document participatory initiatives and gaining access to a database of inspiring examples and methods.

Key Considerations for Publishers

  • Participedia contains three entry types: cases, methods, and organizations. Read more about entry types in the FAQs
  • Like Wikipedia, Participedia is focused on documentation rather than persuasion or opinion.
  • All content must be cited so readers can fact-check original sources.
  • Contributions should draw from credible sources whenever they are available, such as academic papers, reports, official evaluations, official websites of organizations involved, or news outlets.
  • New entries are automatically translated into all supported languages, but only the first time they are published. Read more about how translations work in the FAQs.
  • Write in the third person, not the first person.

Editors

Editors could be adding additional information to a case, method or organization such as citations, images, links to supporting documents or external websites. Most often, people who edit existing entries on Participedia are students and other researchers. Course assignments related to participedia often involve improving partially complete entries, or “stubs.” Researchers may be editors on entries that are related to their particular area of focus.

The Participedia Team additionally includes staff editors, who review the content on Participedia, curate collections, and flag entries with editing prompts such as completeness and required citations for improvement by Participedia’s community of editing contributors.

Key Considerations for Editors

  • On the search page, use the search filter called “entry completeness” to find entries listed as stubs or partially complete, as well as entries that need citations. For example here are cases that are flagged as stubs.
  • Add information or images related to a participatory event you attended (ie: protest, participatory budgeting event, etc)
  • Use search filters to look for entries near you and conduct research to add more content to those entries. For example an entry might need images, links to supporting documents, or external websites.
  • Use the site in your preferred language. All fixed data changes are applied across all languages, and text changes are applied in your language only. Read more about how translations works in the FAQs

We look forward to welcoming you as a publisher or editor on Participedia.net! Once you get started, you can find all your contributions on your profile page.
